Dog Vaccination Plans

Vaccination plans for dogs are designed to protect them from a variety of infectious diseases. The core vaccines are typically recommended for all dogs, while non-core vaccines may be recommended based on the dog's lifestyle, geographic location, and specific risk factors.

Core Vaccines:

  • Canine Distemper Virus (CDV)
  • Canine Parvovirus (CPV)
  • Canine Adenovirus (CAV-2, which protects against infectious hepatitis)
  • Rabies

Non-Core Vaccines:

  • Bordetella bronchiseptica (kennel cough)
  • Leptospira species (Leptospirosis)
  • Borrelia burgdorferi (Lyme disease)
  • Canine Influenza
  • Canine Parainfluenza Virus

Puppy Vaccination Schedule (General Guide):

  • 6-8 weeks:
    • DHPP (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parainfluenza, Parvovirus)
  • 10-12 weeks:
    • DHPP (second dose)
    • Bordetella (if recommended)
  • 14-16 weeks:
    • DHPP (third dose)
    • Rabies (first dose)
    • Leptospirosis (if recommended)
    • Lyme disease (if recommended)
  • 12-16 months:
    • DHPP (booster)
    • Rabies (booster)
    • Leptospirosis (booster if recommended)
    • Lyme disease (booster if recommended)

    • Adult Dog Vaccination Schedule:

    • Every 1-3 years:
      • DHPP (booster every 1-3 years depending on vet recommendation)
      • Rabies (booster every 1-3 years depending on local laws and vaccine used)
      • Leptospirosis (annual booster if recommended)
      • Bordetella (every 6-12 months if at risk)
      • Lyme disease (annual booster if recommended)
      • Canine Influenza (annual booster if recommended)

    Notes:

    • The exact schedule and vaccines required can vary based on factors such as the dog's age, health status, lifestyle, and local disease prevalence.
    • Always consult with your veterinarian to determine the best vaccination plan for your dog.

    Special Considerations for Different Breeds:

    • Some breeds, such as Rottweilers and Doberman Pinschers, are more susceptible to parvovirus and may need more frequent vaccination.
    • Small breed puppies may have different needs regarding the timing and dosage of vaccines due to their size and development rate.

    Factors Influencing Non-Core Vaccine Decisions:

    • Bordetella: Recommended for dogs frequently exposed to other dogs (e.g., boarding, grooming, dog parks).
    • Leptospirosis: Recommended in areas where the disease is common, particularly for dogs exposed to wildlife or standing water.
    • Lyme disease: Recommended in areas where ticks are prevalent.
    • Canine Influenza: Recommended for dogs in areas with known outbreaks or those frequently exposed to other dogs.

    Vaccination plans should always be tailored to the individual dog's needs and should be regularly reviewed and updated in consultation with a veterinarian.
